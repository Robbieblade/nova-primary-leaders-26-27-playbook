Section 2

School Self-Evaluation &
Improvement Planning

Accurate self-evaluation against the Ofsted Toolkit, clear priorities, and a structured action plan to drive improvement.

Self-Evaluation

Knowing your school honestly and robustly

Each school evaluates against the Ofsted Toolkit, assessing every statement criterion, clarifying the process used and the evidence to justify each judgement.

Where possible, this should be quantifiable and measurable against national benchmarks and statistical neighbours. The information behind each assessment is used to make an overall category judgement.

1

Assess against statements

Each school assesses against every Ofsted Toolkit criterion, with evidence justifying whether it is met or not.

2

Overall category judgement

Safeguarding: Met or Not Met. All other areas: Urgent Improvement, Needs Attention, Expected, Strong or Exceptional.

3

Nova Self-Evaluation Tool

Schools input their self-evaluation through the Nova SET, which automates a summary matrix, summary narrative and improvement priorities.

4

Self-Evaluation Summary

Schools approaching or within the Ofsted window produce an SEF Summary using a centralised AI mega-prompt to capture the correct tone and content for Ofsted.

Ofsted category descriptors

  • Safeguarding: Met or Not Met
  • Urgent Improvement
  • Needs Attention
  • Expected
  • Strong
  • Exceptional

Schools approaching the Ofsted window receive external support from a phase-specific practising Ofsted inspector external to Nova Education Trust.

Improvement Planning

From evaluation to action

The self-evaluation is completed through the Nova Self-Evaluation Tool, which generates an action plan with a number of priorities linked to the Ofsted areas.

Schools can also create their own action plans for areas they want to focus on and develop specifically, alongside the priorities generated by the Nova Self-Evaluation Tool.

Progress against action plan targets is assessed half-termly by the headteacher. This informs updated self-evaluation, which may lead to priorities being adjusted or changed.

Priorities linked to Ofsted areas

The Nova Self-Evaluation Tool generates priorities that align directly with the Ofsted inspection framework categories, ensuring improvement planning is grounded in the same evidence base used during inspections.
