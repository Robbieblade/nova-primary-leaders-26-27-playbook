Section 6

Achievement & Assessment

Robust, routine assessment that drives teaching. Three guiding questions, one common system, and clear expectations for every child.

The guiding questions

Three questions every teacher must be able to answer

Which children are not on track?

Either below age-related expectations or below their individual target. This must be known before every lesson, every pupil progress meeting.

What is the specific gap or barrier?

The precise piece of learning missing, or the barrier preventing progress — not a vague sense, but a named gap linked to a teaching action.

What are we doing about it?

The targeted action being taken in teaching, practice or intervention. Assessment without action is just data collection.

Teachers should ask: “What will children know, understand or be able to do better by the end of the lesson?” Goals come from the Nova Playbooks for reading, writing and maths; from the school’s phonics scheme for phonics; and from the pupil’s starting point for those on alternative pathways.

Point in Time Assessment

PITA — the 1–6 Trust-wide scale

Every pupil must have a current PITA score and a target PITA score for the end of the year, recorded in Tracker+.

1
Alternative Pathway

Children who will not be entered into a statutory assessment.

2
Working Significantly Below

Significant curriculum content adaptations needed; accessing learning outside their Key Stage.

3
Not on Track

Some gaps in foundational knowledge; unlikely to meet the standard in the next statutory assessment.

4
On Track

May still have some weaknesses, but overall on track to meet the standard in the next statutory assessment.

5
On Track Plus

No gaps; on track to exceed the standard. Based on what has been taught and expected for their year group.

6
Working Beyond

Routinely accessing learning outside their Key Stage or Phase of Key Stage 2.

Where a pupil is below PITA 4 or below their target, the class teacher must add a short note in Tracker+ explaining: what the pupil can currently do, where the key gaps are, and what needs to happen next. These notes are the basis of every pupil progress meeting.

Daily tools

The tools every school uses as part of daily practice

Showbie

For in-lesson assessment of all pupils. Teachers use heat maps and AI insights to identify understanding and misconceptions in real time.

Magma Maths

For low-stakes assessment combined with personalised follow-up questions. Follow-ups used in class, at home or during transition times.

TT Rockstars

For multiplication fluency in preparation for the Year 4 Multiplication Check. Practice directed by heat maps, not set as routine homework.

Microsoft Reading Progress

Used to assess reading fluency, accuracy and prosody, with key information recorded in Tracker+.

Kaligo

Used alongside teacher observation to identify handwriting and transcription issues. Helps separate transcription barriers from composition goals.

Quest Progress (KS2)

All KS2 pupils complete termly Quest Progress assessments in arithmetic, mathematical reasoning, reading, and SPAG. Standardised Age Scores added to Tracker+.

What to avoid

What schools must not do

Assessment pitfalls

  • Assessment only at the end of a unit
  • Recording data without action
  • Whole-class reteaching based on assumptions
  • Repeating practice without targeting gaps
  • Overcomplicating systems

Data collection 2026–27

Achievement information captured through Tracker+, consistently across all Trust schools. Reading, Writing and Maths recorded using the 6-category PITA system, tracking progress towards the next statutory assessment point.

Summative assessment grades must be inputted into Tracker+ by the Friday of the penultimate week of term.
