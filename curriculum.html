Curriculum

Aligned curriculum principles shaped through Playbooks, the Botherdness approach and shared expectations for what progress looks like across all Nova primaries.

Curriculum Principles

A learning journey, not a coverage exercise

In Nova Primaries, aligned curriculum principles are shaped through Playbooks. The curriculum is not something to cover and evidence — it is a learning journey.

📚

Subject Playbooks

Playbooks exist for Reading, Writing, Maths, History and Geography. Science, Design, Art, RE and Computing to follow in 2026–27. These share the key disciplinary and substantive skills, knowledge and concepts developed during a child’s time at a Nova Primary.

🏫

School contextualisation

Each school contextualises how the learning is delivered. The contexts may vary, but expectations about what progress looks like within a subject are shared across the Trust — enabling moderation, shared CPD and support.

💡

Guerrilla Education

Since 2025 Nova has partnered with Jonathon Lear (Guerrilla Education) to deliver the Unique Schools Proposition through curriculum design. Work with Curriculum Leads directly informs Curriculum-focused QA visits.

📱

Digital curriculum capture

Outcomes can be captured digitally and uploaded to Showbie so the curriculum journey is clear, misconceptions can be identified and feedback provided in multiple ways. In Maths, the majority of work is completed in Magma Maths.

The Botherdness Approach

From summer 2026, Nova began a partnership with Hywel Roberts to implement Botherdness into the ILP and curriculum. Botherdness places stories at the heart of the curriculum to create people in a place with children taking on a role in understanding and solving the problem.

This provides a call to action and context for learning, ensuring disciplinary skills and substantive knowledge are grounded in the universals — so learning feels real, can be accessed by all, and supports children’s understanding of philosophical concepts.

Each school has access to the Botherdness CPD platform, Nova-specific Botherdness webinars and AI planning tools. In each school a Botherdness lead attends Hywel’s 5-day training programme.

Therapeutic Learning Environments

Intentional spaces that support wellbeing

All Nova Primaries should be intentional about the choices they make to ensure that communal spaces, breakout spaces and classrooms follow the principles of the Therapeutic Learning Environments Playbook.

Therapeutic Learning Environments is now a dedicated section of the Playbook. Read the Therapeutic Learning Environments guidance.
