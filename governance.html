Section 3

Internal Governance &
Quality Assurance

A regular, developmental QA schedule ensures consistency across all Nova primaries, with a clear timeline for every visit.

QA Schedule

Termly QA visits across every school

Each primary school has two externally-supported QA visits per year across Behaviour, Teaching Strategies (ILP Strands) and Curriculum & Achievement, plus termly SEND and Safeguarding QAs.

Behaviour (inc. PD)

Evaluating behaviour culture, routines, personal development provision and the implementation of the Behaviour Playbook.

Teaching & Learning (ILP)

Evaluating the quality of direct instruction, independent practice, assessment and feedback through the ILP framework.

Curriculum & Achievement

Evaluating curriculum coherence, intent, implementation, subject leadership and pupil outcomes.

SEND QA

A focused quality assurance visit every term to look at the quality of provision for all SEND children. Led by the Trust Primary SEND Lead once per year.

Safeguarding QA

A focused quality assurance visit every term to look at Safeguarding culture and compliance. Led by the Trust Safeguarding Lead.

A focused Governance QA is undertaken in the autumn term for all primary schools, led by the Nova Governance Lead. Schools within six months of the Ofsted window receive additional external support from a practising phase-specific inspector.

TSIMs & CEO Visits

Three layers of leadership oversight

TSIMs (half-termly)

The DOSI meets the HT for two hours. Focus: SEF evidence, SIF priorities, barriers and risks, and a surface-level walk of the school community. Record of visit shared within 48 hours.

HT Catch-Up (half-termly)

Between TSIMs: operational issues first, then headteacher coaching. Not formally recorded but actions agreed by email. Ongoing coaching also provided via phone, Teams or in person.

CEO Visits (termly)

Ash Rahman visits each term to quality-assure the school environment and culture, engaging with the school community to gather staff and pupil voice.

All QA findings inform the school SEF, written against the Ofsted framework categories. The SEF and Primary Dashboard are used by the DOSI Primary to report to the Exec through ESB and TSB and to Trustees through papers to the Education Board.
